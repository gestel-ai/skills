# Platform Freshness Notes (VERIFY BEFORE USE)

> **These are freshness-sensitive leads, not current facts.** Each item below
> describes a platform mechanic that was reported around 2025–2026. Ad-platform
> rules, feature names, campaign types, and policies change frequently. Do
> **not** state any of these as current in a deliverable unless the user
> supplies dated research or a live lookup confirming it. Use them to know
> *what to check for*, then verify and date-stamp.

When citing any item, restate it as: "As of <date the user verified>, <claim>"
— or drop it. If unverifiable, omit rather than assert.

## What to look for, by platform

**Meta**

- Creative clustering / near-duplicate suppression: ads above a similarity
  threshold may be suppressed, so genuine conceptual diversity matters more than
  raw ad count. Verify the current threshold and feature name before quoting.
- Unified/automated shopping campaign type (renamed over time): check whether a
  competitor runs the automated shopping type vs manual campaigns.

**Google**

- Video-action campaigns have been folded into a newer multi-format campaign
  type: check competitor adoption of the current format.
- AI-driven Search campaign type and an expanded Ads Transparency Center:
  confirm what data the Transparency Center currently exposes.

**TikTok**

- Creative Center benchmarking and AI-generated creative variations at scale:
  assess whether competitor creative looks AI-generated vs original.
- TikTok Shop competitor analysis (storefronts, catalogs, reviews, live).

**LinkedIn**

- Thought Leader Ads scope (employee vs external creators) and CRM-integration
  audience matching: check whether competitors sponsor external thought leaders
  or show precise CRM-matched targeting.

**Microsoft**

- New conversational/Copilot-chat ad placements and CTV inventory: check whether
  competitors appear in these surfaces.

**Apple Ads**

- Branding and naming have changed over time; custom product-page (CPP)
  competitive variants and automated/maximize-conversions bidding: check whether
  competitors run multiple CPP variants or automated bidding.

## Trademark / brand-bidding rules

Whether you may bid on a competitor's brand terms (and whether they may bid on
yours) is governed by each platform's trademark policy, which varies by region
and changes. Always verify the current rule before recommending or acting on
competitor-brand bidding.
